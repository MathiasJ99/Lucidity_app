Our step by step process.

Look below to see the 4 simple steps in our process

  1. Decide what you want to trademark

    With PLACEHOLDER, you can apply for two common trademark types: word trademarks or image trademarks. These can be individual words, phrases, slogans, logos, stylised words, or any combination of these. Images will need to be uploaded in JPG format.

    Top Tips

    • Be unique! Your trademark cannot describe the goods or services it will relate to, for example the word ‘textile’ cannot be a trade mark for a textile company. Made up words, words that are completely unrelated to the goods or services, and highly stylised logo designs often face less difficulties in being registered.
    • Your trademark cannot be offensive or misleading, for example using swear words or using the word ‘organic’ for goods that are not organic.
    • Avoid overly common and non-distinctive text, for example a statement like ‘we are experts’.
    • You may not be able to register your trade mark if it’s the same as, or similar to, a trade mark of another business that sells the same goods or services.

    Contact us if you need any further guidance on any of the above!

  2. Decide what goods and services you wish to include in your registration

    When applying for a trademark, you’ll need to use the classification system to specify the goods and/or services associated with it.

    Our AI powered UK trademark classification search software suggests the most appropriate products and services for your application, making the process quick, simple and hassle-free.

    Top Tips

    • Consider the question 'what do people pay your business for?’. Just because your business involves certain products or services doesn’t mean that you are providing them commercially under your trademark. For example, if you sell sports clothing via your website, you do not need to cover “e-commerce software” but instead you need to focus on “sports clothing” and similar.
    • What goods do you currently sell, or are intending to sell in the future? We recommend that your specification covers all the goods or services you plan to offer within the next five years.
    • Cover a combination of suitable specific terms and more general terms. For example, if your business is a design consultancy, in addition to ‘design services’, consider also including more specific services that your business may provide such as ‘graphic design’, ‘website design’, or ‘product design’.

  3. Provide details of the trademark owner

    You will need to provide the full name of the owner (individual or business) and also the full address. If you are the director of a business filing a new trade mark application relating to the business, we would recommend having the business as the owner of the trade mark.

  4. Confirm and pay

    You will be able to review all the details of the new trade mark application and pay securely online through the Stripe online payment software.

    Following this, you will receive email confirmation of your order and then your trademark application will be filed within 48 hours. You will receive email confirmation of your trademark application having been filed and you will receive any updates along the way to registration. If any issues or objections arise, we will aim to assist you wherever possible, free of charge.
